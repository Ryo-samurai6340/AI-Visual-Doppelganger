    FAQ

    Q: Which image formats are compatible with the replication process?
    A: The replication feature supports images in JPG, PNG, and WEBP formats.
    Q: How does the replication process function?
    A: The replication process begins with your upload of the desired image. Subsequently, you will be able to view both the original image and its replicated image, enabling observation of the effectiveness of the replication process.
    Q: How long does the replication process take?
    A: The duration of the replication process varies based on factors, such as the image complexity and the total dimension of the images being processed. Generally, it takes a few minutes to complete.
    Q: Why am I unable to download?
    A: Please ensure that you have uploaded an image and completed the replication process. Following these steps is essential before initiating the download.
    Q: Why am I experiencing errors during the replication process?
    A: Errors may occur during replication due to network issues. Slow speeds or intermittent connectivity can disrupt the process, leading to errors. To avoid this, ensure a stable internet connection before replicating images. Clearing your browser cache and restarting the process may also help resolve temporary network issues.
    Q: Is the replication feature compatible with all devices?
    A: The replication feature is optimized for various devices. However, users may encounter limitations on iOS devices, especially iPhone and iPad, due to differences in capabilities and browser specifications. Continuous efforts are underway to enhance compatibility across all platforms.
    Q: Is there a limit on the size of images that can be replicated?
    A: Yes, unfortunately. If the sum of the width and height of an image exceeds 1875 pixels, the replication process may encounter errors. To ensure optimal results, using images with dimensions totaling (width + height) 1875 pixels or less is recommended.
    Q: Why haven't I received a reply after more than one business day?
    A: If you submitted a contact form from an iOS device, especially an iPhone or iPad, you might fail the submission. Please ensure you receive an auto-reply email after submission. If not, try again using a different OS, such as Windows or Android devices.
